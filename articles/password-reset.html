Overview

This guide covers three methods for resetting your password, depending on your current access level. Try self-service first ‚Äî it's the fastest option and available 24/7.

Method 1: Self-Service Password Reset (Recommended)

Use this method if you can access the password reset portal. Available 24/7 without IT involvement.

1
Navigate to the password reset portal

Go to https://passwordreset.yourcompany.com or click "Forgot Password" on the login screen.

2
Verify your identity

Enter your work email address and answer the security questions you set up during onboarding.

3
Complete MFA verification

Approve the push notification on your authenticator app, or enter the 6-digit code.

4
Create your new password

Your new password must meet the following requirements:

  • Minimum 12 characters
  • At least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one number
  • Cannot match any of your last 10 passwords
  • Cannot contain your name or username
5
Update saved passwords

After resetting, update your password on all devices (laptop, phone, tablet) and in any applications that use single sign-on.

Method 2: Google Workspace Password Reset

If your organization uses Google Workspace, you can reset via your Google account.

1
Go to accounts.google.com

Click "Forgot password?" below the sign-in form.

2
Follow the Google recovery flow

Google will verify your identity via your recovery email, phone number, or a previously-used device.

3
Set a new password

Follow the organization's password policy (see requirements above).

Method 3: Contact IT Help Desk

If self-service isn't working or your account is locked, contact IT directly.

2

Provide your employee ID, full name, department, and answer verification questions.

3

IT will issue a temporary password. You will be required to change it immediately upon login.

MFA Recovery

If you've lost access to your MFA device (phone, hardware token), you'll need IT assistance:

  • Contact the help desk and request an MFA reset
  • IT will verify your identity with enhanced verification (in-person ID or video call)
  • A new MFA enrollment will be triggered on your next login
  • Set up your new MFA device immediately
üí° Pro Tip: Always save your MFA backup codes in a secure location (password manager or locked drawer). This prevents lockout if you lose your primary MFA device.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need to change my password?

Per our security policy, passwords must be changed every 90 days. You'll receive email reminders starting 14 days before expiration.

My account is locked after too many failed attempts. What do I do?

Accounts lock after 5 failed attempts for 30 minutes. If you need immediate access, contact the help desk for a manual unlock.

Can I use a password manager?

Yes! We strongly encourage using an approved password manager. Contact IT for the list of approved tools.

What if I think my password was compromised?

Immediately change your password and report the incident to IT Security. We will investigate and take additional protective measures if needed.

Still need help? Submit a ticket in GLPI under Account Access ‚Üí Password Reset or call the IT Help Desk at ext. 4357.
