Overview

This guide covers common VPN connection issues for remote healthcare workers. Our organization uses a split-tunnel VPN configuration ‚Äî only traffic to internal resources routes through the VPN, while general internet traffic goes directly through your home network.

Before You Start: Quick Checks

‚úì
Verify your internet connection

Open a browser and navigate to google.com. If that fails, the issue is your internet connection, not the VPN.

‚úì
Check VPN client is installed and updated

Look for the VPN icon in your system tray (Windows) or menu bar (Mac). If missing, reinstall from the Software Center.

‚úì
Confirm your credentials

VPN uses the same credentials as your work account. If you recently changed your password, update it in the VPN client.

Common Issues & Solutions

Issue 1: "Connection Timed Out"

Cause: Firewall blocking VPN traffic, ISP restrictions, or server unreachable.

1
Try a different VPN server

Switch between available servers in the VPN client settings (e.g., vpn1.yourcompany.com ‚Üí vpn2.yourcompany.com).

2
Check your firewall settings

Ensure ports 443 (SSL) and 1194 (OpenVPN) are not blocked by your router or security software.

3
Restart your network stack

Disable Wi-Fi, wait 10 seconds, re-enable. Or restart your router/modem if the issue persists.

4
Try a mobile hotspot

If your home network blocks VPN, test with a phone hotspot to confirm. If hotspot works, the issue is your home network.

Issue 2: "Authentication Failed"

Cause: Wrong password, expired credentials, or account lockout.

1
Verify your credentials

Log in to webmail.yourcompany.com with the same credentials. If that fails, your password may have changed or expired.

2
Reset your password

See How to Reset Your Password for instructions.

3
Check for account lockout

After 5 failed VPN attempts, your account locks for 30 minutes. Wait or call the help desk for an unlock.

Issue 3: "Connected but Can't Access Internal Resources"

Cause: DNS resolution failure, split tunneling misconfiguration, or server-side access issue.

1
Verify VPN is fully connected

Check the VPN client shows "Connected" status and an assigned internal IP (typically 10.x.x.x or 172.16.x.x).

2
Flush DNS cache

Windows: ipconfig /flushdns | Mac: sudo dscacheutil -flushcache

3
Try accessing by IP address

If ehr.internal doesn't resolve, try the direct IP. If IP works but hostname doesn't, it's a DNS issue ‚Äî contact IT.

4
Disconnect, reconnect

Fully disconnect the VPN, wait 10 seconds, and reconnect. This forces a fresh route table update.

Issue 4: "VPN is Slow / High Latency"

Cause: Network congestion, distance from server, or bandwidth limitations.

  • Run a speed test at speedtest.net with VPN off, then with VPN on. A 10-20% drop is normal.
  • Connect to the geographically closest VPN server.
  • Close bandwidth-heavy applications (streaming, large downloads).
  • If on Wi-Fi, move closer to the router or switch to a wired (Ethernet) connection.
  • If consistently slow (>50% speed reduction), contact IT ‚Äî the VPN server may need capacity adjustments.
