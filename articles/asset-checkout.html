Asset Check-Out / Check-In Process

Last updated: January 2026 ‚Ä¢ 4 min read ‚Ä¢ Audience: All Staff

Overview

All IT equipment is tracked in Snipe-IT, our IT Asset Management system. Every laptop, monitor, phone, and accessory has a unique asset tag. When you receive, return, or transfer equipment, it must be logged in Snipe-IT. This ensures we know where every device is, who has it, and when it needs maintenance or replacement.

üì± Why Asset Tracking Matters: In healthcare environments, HIPAA requires organizations to track devices that may contain or access Protected Health Information (PHI). Proper asset tracking helps us maintain compliance and respond quickly to security incidents.

Requesting Equipment

1
Submit a request through GLPI

Navigate to the self-service portal and submit a ticket under Hardware ‚Üí Equipment Request.

Include:

  • Type of equipment needed (laptop, monitor, headset, etc.)
  • Business justification (new hire, replacement, project need)
  • Preferred pickup location or shipping address
  • Needed-by date
2
Manager approval

Your manager will receive an approval request. Standard equipment (laptop, monitor) is typically auto-approved. Non-standard items (tablets, specialized hardware) require explicit approval.

3
IT prepares equipment

IT will configure the device (imaging, software installation, security settings) and update Snipe-IT with the assignment. Typical lead time: 1-3 business days.

Checking Out Equipment (Receiving)

1
Meet with IT for equipment handoff

IT will provide the equipment and walk you through basic setup. All items will have an asset tag (barcode sticker) ‚Äî do not remove this tag.

2
Sign the equipment acknowledgment form

Review and sign the form confirming:

  • You received the listed equipment in working condition
  • You understand you're responsible for the equipment's care
  • You'll return it when requested or upon departure
  • You'll report damage, loss, or theft immediately
3
IT records the checkout in Snipe-IT

The asset is checked out to your name with the current date. You can view your assigned assets anytime in the Snipe-IT self-service portal.

Returning Equipment (Check-In)

1
Back up your personal data

Before returning any device, save personal files to Google Drive or an external drive. IT will wipe the device after check-in.

2
Contact IT to schedule return

Submit a GLPI ticket under Hardware ‚Üí Equipment Return or bring the equipment directly to the IT office. Include all accessories (charger, dock, cables, bag).

3
IT inspects and checks in the asset

IT will verify the condition, note any damage, and check the asset back into Snipe-IT. The equipment goes back into available inventory or is flagged for repair/retirement.

Reporting Lost, Stolen, or Damaged Equipment

üö® Lost or Stolen Device: Report immediately ‚Äî within 1 hour if the device may contain PHI. Delays increase HIPAA breach risk and may require formal breach notification.
1
Call IT immediately

Call ext. 4357 and specify "lost/stolen device." Do not wait to submit a ticket ‚Äî call first.

2
IT will remotely wipe the device

If the device accessed PHI or email, IT will initiate a remote wipe to protect patient data.

3
File a police report (theft only)

For stolen equipment, file a police report and provide the report number to IT.

4
IT updates Snipe-IT

The asset status is updated to Lost/Stolen with incident details, police report number, and date.

Transferring Equipment Between Employees

Equipment should never be passed directly between employees. All transfers must go through IT to ensure proper tracking:

  1. The current holder checks the equipment back in to IT
  2. IT inspects, wipes (if needed), and reconfigures for the new user
  3. The new user checks the equipment out through the standard process

Viewing Your Assigned Assets

To see what equipment is assigned to you, log in to the Snipe-IT self-service portal at assets.yourcompany.com. Your dashboard shows all checked-out assets with asset tags, serial numbers, and checkout dates.

Need equipment? Submit a request in GLPI under Hardware ‚Üí Equipment Request. Returning or reporting issues? Call ext. 4357 or visit the IT office.
